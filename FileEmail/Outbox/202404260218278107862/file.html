Very well, Ms Kusuma Marisya. We inform several advantages of the GNSS-based MLFF system include:
1. Toll road users do not stop while paying, without reducing vehicle speed on the toll road
2. Prevent congestion in payments at toll booths
3. Can be downloaded on smart devices as Electronic On Board Unit or e-OBU
4. Can be used in real time without depending on time and weather
5. The use of GNSS in determining relative position is not affected by the topographical conditions of the area
7. The specified position refers to global time which is relatively precise and easy to realize
8. Provides positional accuracy with a fairly wide spectrum
9. No fees
10. Efficient in terms of time, operational costs and energy
11. Data manipulation in GNSS is more difficult

Weaknesses of MLFF, as follows:
1. GNSS-based MLFF relies heavily on GSM (3G/4G/5G) cellular networks along toll roads
2. There must be an additional OBU on the vehicle or e-OBU on the smartphone
3. Not all toll road users have mastered IT technology on GNSS-based smartphones
4. There is always a balance in the e-wallet
5. Always have internet data on your smart device when on the toll road

However, as time and technology develop, some of these weaknesses will be overcome with further outreach so that other people can easily use the MLFF system.
